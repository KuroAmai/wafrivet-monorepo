# Wafrivet Website Content & IA Document (v2)

This document contains copy and structural guidance for the Wafrivet marketing site. It is written to match the existing brand tone and hero design while positioning Wafrivet as **the OS for Livestock Agriculture**.

---

## 1. Global UX & Copy Principles

Use these across all pages so the site feels consistent and intentional.

- **Plain language first.** Talk like you would to a smart farmer or vet in person. Avoid buzzwords unless they are explained.
- **Outcome-over-feature.** Lead with results (fewer deaths, faster drugs, less chaos), then explain how the tech works.
- **One primary CTA per page.** e.g. "Get Started", "Book a Demo", or "Talk to Sales". Secondary CTAs can be "Explore for Farmers" etc.
- **Short sections, strong headings.** Most blocks should be scannable in 5 seconds.
- **Use bento-style layouts.** Group related ideas into tiles/blocks (hardware, AI, marketplace, channels) so the ecosystem feels simple, not overwhelming.
- **Reuse proof often.** Surface metrics, logos, testimonials near CTAs.

Tone: calm, confident, practical, slightly conversational.

### 3.4 "How Wafrivet Works" Section

Use a simple step flow or horizontal timeline.

**Heading:**  
> How Wafrivet works — from tag to treatment.

**Steps (5):**
1. **Tag the animals.**  
   Plug‑and‑play ear tags and collars start streaming temperature, movement, and location data.

2. **AI watches the herd.**  
   Wafrivet looks for early signs of fever, behaviour change, or zone‑level clustering that signal trouble.

3. **You get clear alerts.**  
   We tell you *which* animals or houses are at risk, *why*, and how urgent it is — via app, WhatsApp, or USSD.

4. **Act in minutes, not weeks.**  
   Talk to an AI assistant or a vet, isolate animals, and order the right drugs or vaccines immediately.

5. **Everything is recorded automatically.**  
   Every alert, visit, treatment, and movement updates the animal’s NFC record and your farm history.
